APPLICATION FOR RELIEF OTHER THAN IN RESPECT OF ERROR OR MISTAKE UNDER SECTION 131A OF THE ITA

An application for relief for other than error or mistake case is allowed for the following circumstances:
any exemption, reliefs, remissions, allowance or deduction granted under the ITA or any written law gazetted after the year of assessment in which the Income Tax Return Form (ITRF) was furnished; or
approval for any exemption, relief, remission, allowance or deduction is granted after the year of assessment in which the ITRF was furnished; OR
a deduction not allowed in respect of payment not due to be paid under subsection 107A(2) or 109(2), section 109A, or subsection 109B(2) or 109F(2) on the day the ITRF was furnished.
The conditions for an application for relief under section 131A of the ITA are:
the taxpayer must furnish the ITRF in accordance with subsection 77(1) or 77A(1) of the ITA;
the taxpayer has paid all taxes that have been assessed for the relevant year of assessment.
The taxpayer must make an application for relief by a letter or a Form CP15F within the stipulated time as follows:
for the application for relief in subparagraphs 5(a) and (b), within five (5) years after the end of the year in which the exemption, relief, remission, allowance or deduction is published in the Gazette, or the approval was granted, whichever is later; AND
for the relief application in subparagraph 5(c), within one (1) year after the end of the year in which payment was made.
